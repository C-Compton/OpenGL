00103 /*
00104  * The different codes wrt. to Linux
00105  * is a bit hard to explain.
00106  * Looking at Bluetooth CoD format, we have 24 bits.
00107  * In wiic Linux they are stored in three fields,
00108  * each one 8bit long. The result number is
00109  * 0x002504. However, MacOS Bluetooth does
00110  * not store them in such a way, rather it uses
00111  * the concept of major service, major class,
00112  * and minor class, that are respectivelly
00113  * 11bit, 5bit, and 6bit long. Hence, the 
00114  * numbers are different.
00115  * The Wiimote CoD Bluetooth division is the following:
00116  * 00000000001 00101 000001 00 (major service - major class - minor class - format type)
00117  * This can also be seen in the WiiC Linux way:
00118  * 00000000 00100101 00000100 
00119  */
